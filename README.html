Phase of Exponential Growth

Currently, all countries in Europe are in the epidemic phase of were the increase of incidence can be described by a law of exponential growth. epidemiologist may write Id=I0 * Cd, where Id, I0 are the number of infected people at day d and the initial value of infected people. The factor C contains the average number E of people, an infected person is exposed to each day, and the propability p of each exposure becoming an infection. The exponential growth can be slowed down, when E and/or p can be minimised. Instead of C, Physicists would use the exponential base e: Id=I0 * e(k*d). The logarithmic plot of cumulative incidences ln(Id/I0)=k*d over days yield a straight line, with the slope k.
